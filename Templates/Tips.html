How to get started

Creating a conlang can be a daunting task, especially if you haven't made one before. One of the most common questions we get asked is "Where do I start?". The truth is, there really isn't a right answer! Each of our team members has had a different approach, and in our community we similarly see a wide range of how people start making their language.

That answer, though, isn't helpful, we know! So we collected some answers from our team members to try and give you an idea of ways you can start making your conlang!

Mod Shiro
"I wasn't that familiar with linguistics, so I started by using Vulgar to generate a language, and messed around with the program until I started to get a feel for what I wanted my language to be."
Mod Ximena
"I didn't care too much about the linguistics side of things, so I just used English letters and sounds and used a word generator to create words."
Mod Abdullah
"I started out by figuring out what sounds I wanted in my language, then made groups of syllables , then how I wanted to structure my words, and from there it was pretty easy to make words."
